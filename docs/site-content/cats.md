# Cats Page

## Content

### Top section, below header

I'm a cat lover. My love for cats goes back to childhood, when our family had a rather ornery cat who lived to be nearly 19. The story goes that when we went to pick her out, I chose the one huddled in the corner, while her littermates went up to us seeking affection. And she lived up that early experience. She'd bite if you pet her too much, and she'd rarely approach us except for food. But-I loved that cat. Honestly, looking back, I wish I'd followed her example more and not tried as much to be a "people pleaser." She (should have) taught me how to live life with my head (or tail) held high, not caring about what other people think. Regardless, she was a great pet.

### Middle section, below Mal picture

My next cat came into my life at a very different time. I loved in Boston for two years during a particularly dreadful period for me. This was a time when my first profession and first marriage were crumbling. That last fall when I was there, a kitten came into our lives. A friend of a friend found a tiny kitten in a truck engine in Philadelphia, and asked if we would take him. Long story short, we did. He was a great pet and companion. He saw me through eight very intense years. He and I lived in four states together, during a divorce, the death of one career, the dramatic rebirth of another, and eventually meeting my now-wife. He was very affectionate and we had a strong bond.  

[Picture of Jessie]

My current cat is very different than the others. He's very feisty. He can be affectionate, but he often bites and scratches. He's been a handful for us! But we're devoted to him. We believe that when you adopt an animal, it's a lifetime commitment--for their lives, of course. We will stick with him and try to improve his behavior.
